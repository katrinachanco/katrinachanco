Ever After Album Cover Description - Katrina Chanco

My Album Artwork is an interpretation of the Album Cover. The original was showcasing Marianas Trenchâ€™s storyline idea involving the fantasy Toyland. I decided to make a cover based off of how I interpreted the album, and its meaning. The theme of what I analyzed the album to be was; personal growth.

The trees would signify this development as a person goes through life. The larger the tree gets, the more an individual has learned and experienced.

The road leading away is the journey someone goes through, and how we have to go into what is unknown in order to find out more about ourselves.


The font of the lettering is meant to give off a feeling of mystery and whimsy. We cannot predict the future, going through each day in a daze of uncertainty, doing our best to become the best version of ourselves we can be.

The shades varying from white to black is used to show contrast. The Yin and Yang, good and bad, right and wrong of what we encounter in our lives. In order to fully develop, we have to look at all of the aspects of our life, and adapt from there.

The puzzle symbolizes the complexity of human life. We are made of different parts; all of which add up to create who we are today. It may be confusing to sort out, but there is a purpose for everything. Personal growth is messy, but once we get all the pieces to fit with one another; we are able to discover who we are, and who we want to be.
